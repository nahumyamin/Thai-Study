Thai Grammar

Thai grammar is remarkably different from English. There are no verb conjugations, no plural endings, and no articles. Word order and a small set of particles carry most of the grammatical meaning. These ten patterns cover the structures you will encounter most frequently.

1 Basic sentence order: Subject – Verb – Object S + V + O

Thai follows the same subject–verb–object order as English. Verbs do not change form for tense, person, or number. Context, time words, and aspect particles tell you when something happens.

highlighted = key word
ฉันกินข้าว
chan gin khao
I eat rice. (or: I ate rice. — tense from context)
เขาขายเครื่องสำอาง
khao khai khrueang sam ang
She sells cosmetics.
รัฐบาลกำหนดกฎหมาย
rat tha ban kam not kot mai
The government sets the law.
2 Tense & aspect with จะ / กำลัง / แล้ว / เคย particle + verb

There are no verb tenses in Thai. Instead, four particles placed before or after the verb signal when or how an action happens: จะ (future/intention), กำลัง (right now), แล้ว (completed / already), เคย (past experience / used to).

highlighted = tense particle
เขาจะไปวัดพรุ่งนี้
khao cha pai wat phrueng ni
He will go to the temple tomorrow.
ฉันกำลังกินข้าวอยู่
chan kam lang gin khao yu
I am eating right now.
เขาเคยอยู่ในกองทัพ
khao khoei yu nai kong thap
He used to be in the military.
3 Negation with ไม่ ไม่ + verb/adjective

ไม่ (mai, falling tone) is placed directly before a verb or adjective to negate it — equivalent to "not", "don't", or "doesn't". For the verb มี (to have/exist), negation uses ไม่มี. Emphatic negation adds เลย at the end: not at all.

highlighted = negation word
ฉันไม่ชอบอาหารเผ็ด
chan mai chop ahan phet
I don't like spicy food.
ซอยนี้ไม่มีที่กลับรถ
soi ni mai mi thi klap rot
This alley has no turning space.
เขาไม่สนใจเลย
khao mai son chai loei
He is not interested at all.
4 Adjectives follow nouns; comparison with กว่า noun + adjective; A + adj + กว่า + B

In Thai, adjectives come after the noun they describe, unlike English. For comparisons, add กว่า after the adjective: more … than. Superlatives use ที่สุด after the adjective: the most ….

highlighted = adjective / comparison word
ซอยแคบ / บ้านเก่า
soi khaep / ban kao
a narrow alley / an old house
รถไฟแพงกว่ารถทัวร์
rot fai phaeng kwa rot thua
The train is more expensive than the bus.
ผัดไทยเป็นอาหารที่มีชื่อเสียงที่สุด
phat thai pen ahan thi mi chue siang thi sut
Pad thai is the most famous dish.
5 Modals: ต้อง / สามารถ...ได้ / อยาก / ควร / อาจจะ modal + verb (+ ได้)

Thai modals sit before the main verb and do not change form. The most common ones: ต้อง (must), สามารถ…ได้ (can/able to — split around the verb), อยาก (want to), ควร (should), อาจจะ (might). ได้ alone after a verb also means "can" or marks a completed action.

highlighted = modal
คุณต้องจ่ายก่อนวันที่ห้า
khun tong chai kon wan thi ha
You must pay before the 5th.
สามารถถอนเงินได้ที่ตู้เอทีเอ็ม
sa mat thon ngoen dai thi tu ATM
You can withdraw money at an ATM.
เขาอาจจะเป็นทหารก็ได้
khao at cha pen tha han ko dai
He might become a soldier.
6 Causative ทำให้ — "to cause / to make" cause + ทำให้ + effect

ทำให้ (tham hai) is one of the most common structures in written Thai. It connects a cause to its effect, meaning "causes", "makes", or "results in". The subject performs the action that brings about the result stated after ทำให้.

highlighted = ทำให้
หมอกควันทำให้หายใจไม่ออก
mok khwan tham hai hai jai mai ok
The smog makes it hard to breathe.
รถไฟฟ้าทำให้มีคนมาขอซื้อที่ดิน
rot fai fa tham hai mi khon ma kho sue thi din
The Skytrain caused people to come and buy land.
การกินน้ำน้อยทำให้ร่างกายอ่อนแอ
kan gin nam noi tham hai rang kai on ae
Drinking too little water makes the body weak.
7 Conditionals with ถ้า…ก็ — "if … then …" ถ้า + condition + ก็ + result

ถ้า opens the condition clause (if). ก็ starts the result clause (then). ก็ is optional in informal speech but very common in written Thai. The structure works for present, future, and hypothetical situations alike — tense comes from context.

highlighted = conditional words
ถ้าได้ใบแดงก็ต้องเป็นทหาร
tha dai bai daeng ko tong pen tha han
If you draw a red card, then you must serve.
ถ้าซื้อเป็นจำนวนมากก็มีบริการส่งฟรี
tha sue pen cam nuan mak ko mi bo ri kan song fri
If you buy in bulk, there is free delivery.
ถ้าไม่จ่ายก่อนวันที่ห้าก็จะถูกตัดไฟ
tha mai chai kon wan thi ha ko cha thuk tat fai
If you don't pay by the 5th, the power will be cut.
8 Relative clauses with ที่ — "that / which / who" noun + ที่ + verb/clause

ที่ functions as a relative pronoun, equivalent to English "that", "which", or "who". It follows the noun being described and introduces a clause that modifies it. The same word ที่ also means "place", so context matters.

highlighted = ที่
คนไทยที่สูบบุหรี่มีเกือบสิบล้านคน
khon thai thi sup bu ri mi kuea sip lan khon
Thais who smoke number almost ten million.
ความเชื่อที่คนรุ่นก่อนบอกต่อกันมา
khwam chuea thi khon run kon bok to kan ma
Beliefs that past generations passed down.
งานที่คนไทยไม่อยากทำ
ngan thi khon thai mai yak tham
Work that Thais don't want to do.
9 Reporting speech & thought with ว่า verb of saying/thinking + ว่า + clause

ว่า introduces a quoted or reported clause after verbs of saying, thinking, believing, or knowing — like English "that" in "he said that…". There is no change between direct and indirect speech; the verb form stays the same.

highlighted = ว่า
หลายคนบอกว่าโรแมนติกมาก
lai khon bok wa ro maen tik mak
Many people say that it is very romantic.
รัฐบาลยอมรับว่านี่คือปัญหาระดับชาติ
rat tha ban yom rap wa ni khue pan ha ra dap chat
The government admits that this is a national problem.
ฉันเชื่อว่าจะมีทางแก้ไขปัญหาได้
chan chuea wa cha mi thang kae khai pan ha dai
I believe that there will be a solution.
10 Discourse connectors: เพราะ / แต่ / ดังนั้น / อย่างไรก็ตาม clause + connector + clause

These four connectors build the logical flow of sentences. เพราะ gives a reason (because). แต่ introduces a contrast (but). ดังนั้น signals a conclusion (therefore). อย่างไรก็ตาม concedes a point (however). Mastering them dramatically improves reading speed.

highlighted = connector
คนชอบรถไฟเพราะราคาถูก แต่ใช้เวลานาน
khon chop rot fai phro ra kha thuk tae chai we la nan
People like the train because it's cheap, but it takes a long time.
น้ำมีความสำคัญมาก ดังนั้นเราต้องดื่มทุกวัน
nam mi khwam sam khan mak dang nan rao tong dueam thuk wan
Water is very important; therefore we must drink it every day.
ผิดกฎหมาย อย่างไรก็ตามคนก็ยังซื้ออยู่
phit kot mai yang rai ko tam khon ko yang sue yu
It is illegal; however, people still buy it.
11 The five uses of ให้ give · let · for · so that · causative

ให้ (hai) is one of Thai's most versatile words. Its meaning shifts depending on position and surrounding verbs. The same written word carries five distinct grammatical functions — sometimes in the same sentence. Learning to distinguish them is key to reading Thai naturally.

① ให้ = to give (main verb)

When ให้ is the main verb of a sentence, it simply means "to give". The structure is: giver + ให้ + recipient + thing given.

highlighted = ให้
พนักงานให้เงินมา
pha nak ngan hai ngoen ma
The staff gives the money over.
เจ้าภาพให้ของเล็กๆ น้อยๆ แก่แขก
chao phap hai khong lek lek noi noi kae khaek
The host gives small gifts to the guests.
② verb + ให้ + person = to do something for someone (beneficiary)

ให้ after a verb introduces the person who benefits from the action — "do X for Y". The person receiving the benefit follows ให้ directly.

highlighted = ให้
มีบริการมาส่งให้ถึงบ้าน
mi bo ri kan ma song hai thueng ban
There is a delivery service that brings it for you right to your door.
ฉันฝากให้เขาซื้อเครื่องสำอาง
chan fak hai khao sue khrueang sam ang
I asked him to buy cosmetics for me. (entrusted it to him)
③ ให้ + person + verb = to let / to have someone do something (permissive / causative)

When ให้ precedes a person and then a verb, it means "to let", "to allow", or "to have someone do something". This is the permissive-causative pattern. The subject enables or instructs someone else to act.

highlighted = ให้
รัฐบาลสั่งให้ทุกคนอยู่บ้าน
rat tha ban sang hai thuk khon yu ban
The government ordered everyone to stay home. (had everyone stay)
กฎหมายอนุญาตให้ติดป้ายว่ามีบุหรี่ขาย
kot mai a nu yat hai tit pai wa mi bu ri khai
The law allows shops to post a sign saying cigarettes are sold.
รัฐบาลสนับสนุนให้ทุกคนได้เรียนมหาวิทยาลัย
rat tha ban sa nap sa nun hai thuk khon dai rian ma ha wit tha ya lai
The government supports everyone being able to study at university.
④ verb + ให้ + result = so that / in order to achieve a result

After an action verb, ให้ can introduce the intended result or outcome — meaning "so that", "until", or "enough to". It links an action to the state it is supposed to produce.

highlighted = ให้
เอาไปต้มให้เดือดก่อน
ao pai tom hai dueat kon
Take it and boil it until it boils first.
รัฐบาลพยายามลดจำนวนคนสูบบุหรี่ให้น้อยลง
rat tha ban pha ya yam lot cam nuan khon sup bu ri hai noi long
The government tries to reduce the number of smokers so that it goes down.
⑤ ให้ in reported instructions = "to" after verbs of telling / asking

After verbs like บอก (tell), ขอ (ask/request), สั่ง (order), เชิญ (invite), ให้ introduces what was requested or instructed — equivalent to English "to" in "told him to come". Compare this with ว่า (rule 9), which introduces what was said.

highlighted = ให้
ครอบครัวจะเชิญแขกให้มางานศพ
khrob khruea cha choen khaek hai ma ngan sop
The family will invite guests to come to the funeral.
เจ้าหน้าที่จะเรียกคนให้ออกมารับบิล
chao na thi cha riak khon hai ok ma rap bin
The officer will call someone to come out and collect the bill.
Quick reference ① ให้ alone as main verb → give ② verb + ให้ + person → do X for someone ③ verb + ให้ + person + verb → let / have / allow someone to do X ④ verb + ให้ + adjective/result → so that / until / enough to ⑤ tell/ask/invite + ให้ + verb → told/asked/invited to do X

Thai Pronunciation

Thai is a tonal language written in its own script. This guide covers the five tones, the 44 consonants grouped by class, and the main vowel forms — the three things that most directly affect whether you are understood.

Beginner tips

01
Tones matter more than vowels
A foreigner mispronouncing a vowel is usually understood from context. A wrong tone can produce a completely different word — or be incomprehensible. Focus on tones first.
02
Thai has no spaces between words
Sentences are written without spaces — spaces only appear between clauses or sentences. Learning to identify word boundaries comes with exposure, not a rule.
03
Final consonants are unreleased
Syllables ending in p, t, or k are not released — the mouth closes but no puff of air comes out. This is why Thai words can sound like they end abruptly to English ears.
04
ร is often silent or becomes น
In everyday speech, ร (r) at the end of a syllable or in a consonant cluster is often dropped or pronounced as น (n). ไทย sounds like "tai", not "tair".
05
Vowels can be above, below, before, or around
Unlike Latin script, Thai vowel symbols are placed in all directions around the consonant. Reading order is still left-to-right, but you must scan all four positions to identify the vowel.
06
The script has no uppercase
Thai script has no capital letters. Names, sentence beginnings, and titles are not marked by case — only by context and spacing.

Building larger numbers

Tens: สิบ (sip) × digit
สิบ = 10. Add a digit after it for 11–19. For multiples of ten, put the digit before สิบ. Exception: 20 uses ยี่ (yii) not สอง.

ยี่สิบห้าyii sip ha25

Hundreds & thousands
ร้อย (roi) = 100 · พัน (phan) = 1,000 · หมื่น (muen) = 10,000 · แสน (saen) = 100,000 · ล้าน (lan) = 1,000,000. Note: Thai has individual words for 10,000 and 100,000 — they don't say "ten thousand".

Ordinals: ที่ (thi) + number
To make an ordinal (first, second, third…), put ที่ before the number. Alternatively, อันดับ (an dap) is used for rankings.

Numbers in context

Prices
บาท (baht) follows the number. สตางค์ (satang) = cents, rarely used.
สามสิบสองบาท
sam sip song baht — 32 baht
Time
Thai uses a 6-hour clock system informally. นาที = minutes, ชั่วโมง = hours.
สองทุ่ม
song thum — 8pm (2nd evening bell)
Dates & years
Thailand uses the Buddhist Era (พ.ศ.) — add 543 to the Western year. วันที่ = date.
วันที่ห้า / พ.ศ. ๒๕๖๗
the 5th / B.E. 2567 (= 2024)
Percentages & fractions
เปอร์เซ็นต์ = percent. ร้อยละ (roi la) = per hundred (formal). ครึ่ง = half.
ร้อยละสิบเจ็ด
17 percent (formal written style)
Phone & lottery numbers
Digits read one by one. เบอร์ (boe) = number/telephone number. เลข (lek) = number/digit.
เบอร์โทรศัพท์เลขมงคล
an auspicious phone number
Floors & addresses
ชั้น (chan) = floor/level. Thai buildings sometimes skip floor 13 (Western superstition).
ชั้นสิบสาม
13th floor (often absent in buildings)
